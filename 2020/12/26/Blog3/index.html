Equality vs Fairness

All men are created equal, or so reckoned Thomas Jefferson as he drafted American’s Declaration of Independence in 1776. “Equality” has been a vital topic for a long time. By comparison, the situation is the same to “Fairness”. But are the two terms actually identical? In the story Harrison Bergeron, introducing a dystopia society in which the government is too crazy about egalitarianism, the difference among them emerges. The definitions are distinct for fairness is an uncertain process full of emotions and equality, however, represents something people are born with but then still be defined as a life destination. To be more specific, the difference between equality and fairness is related to the subjectivity, quality, and possibility.

As a matter of fact, fairness is much more humanized than equality, which is more subjectivity. The main character of this story, Harrison, wears extremely heavy handicaps, earphones, and spectacles (Vonnegut 3). It is definitely a miserable experience that everyone tries to get away from. To achieve absolute equality, the governor puts little attention on preserving the human liberty of enjoying advantages, and even destroys it. On the contrary, Harrison dances with his empress freely after getting rid of the handicaps. Real fairness needs protecting personal legal rights. It is exactly what Harrison wanted and achieved in the end. Clearly fairness is respectable to human being thus everyone is truly chirrupy. In other words, fairness is reasonable and can better release human nature than equality.

When it comes to the quality, sometimes, people cannot realize the fact that fairness is a process while equality stands for a result. The handicaps and masks for the characters are different methods to ensure everyone’s ability is at the same level. “Fairness” is taken into every government act in this controlling process, preventing the privileges like encouraging ugly masks to the beauties. “Fairness” is a tool in their daily life to achieve the final equality. After so much effort put in, eventually society in the US is “equal” for fairness has developed along the right lines. This phenomenon remains considered as a “satisfied” result. By analyzing the logic and evidence, it turns out to be a truth that fairness is a way of struggling to be equal. They play individual roles in different positions or stages.

Jefferson emphasized “All men are created equal”, somehow fairness exists uncertainly when babies are born in some cases. Obviously, in Harrison Bergeron, everybody leads a mortal life in which they enjoy the rights to do personal things, normal and basic, like watching TV and marrying someone. Basically it just goes to show the equal rights people have. Then turning to George, Harrison’s father. His intelligence is way above normal but the one of his wife’s is pretty normal. Disabilities among babies would certainly condemn them to a life of panic and frustration, but these situations are inevitable. Individualism and differences are always there. In conclusion, equality is supposed to describe the rights everyone has. The rights are given without disregarding anyone, but carrying unfair qualities with them possibly.

All in all, the difference consists of three indispensable parts. Admittedly, it seems that fairness and equality are similar although fairness is more subjective and colorful. We can fight for this and considering it as a faith. Then without plenty of concerns, equality would come in the end itself.

Works cited page

Vonnegut, Kurt Vonnegut, Harrison Bergeron. Delacorte Press, 1968.PDF. Retrieved from http://www.tnellen.com/westside/harrison.pdf
