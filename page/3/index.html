explore unwind part 7

u How does the author keep you in suspense in the first page of this chapter?

Connor wakes up in a medically induced coma, unconscious for two weeks which seems like eternity. His body is wound with bandage and the ache from whole body still doesn’t go away. However, everyone should know that Connor is the key Unwind, he couldn’t be released. Therefore, the nurse who comes to look after him might still someone working for Happy Jack Harvest Camp… Connor and Risa may still under the control of harvest camp and can get unwound later. The whole explosive actions happened before would worth nothing…like a big joke.

u Do you think it is ironic that Connor received the particular transplant that he did?

It seems quite ironic because Connor is the one who said “I would rather die than receive a piece of Unwind” before and he tried to run away from the destiny of getting unwound desperately. But now, he received a eye and an arm during his unconsciousness— he got something he never wants…he got something that comes from the procedure he ever wants to escape from. It must be astonishing and scary for Connor to accept. Yeah, it may seem ironic to Connor himself, but it was not his choice. Actually, this scene changes my attitude towards unwinding quite a lot. You see, Connor is always the one I appreciate, consistently fighting against unwinding, fighting for human rights. But when he is in then danger of dying, our superhero also needs organisms to survive, to fight back again. I think that’s why New York Times gave an approving review, claiming that “the power of the novel lies in what it doesn’t do: come down explicitly on one side or the other.” Now I wouldn’t be so outright like before emphasizing that unwinding is totally an unethical thing to do. I would be more open-minded. Maybe there are no right or wrong for the compromise between pro-life side and pro-choice side. God throws this tuff question to the world, just in aims of awaking us from blindness and staying open-minded. Fight for what you love, and accept the rest. Just as Nurse Greta said, “Love the ones you can, pray for the rest.”

u What does Risa think Connor has changed into (end of chapter)?

Risa thinks Connor has changed into a man from a boy. It isn’t Connor’s age makes him man— it is how much he has grown up. The sense of naïve and impulsive have faded away. Connor’s soul was tamed by his impulsiveness. However, after having gone through so many difficulties, his soul begins to tam any other threats.

u Why does Lev’s lawyer talk to him about ‘insanity like it’s a good thing’?

Because from the lawyer’s point of view, he or she needs to stand by the side of Lev and to protect his life from unwinding. Insanity for becoming a clapper actually saves Lev’s life due to the chemicals in his blood. Therefore, “insanity like it” is a good thing for Lev which get him out of unwinding destiny.

u Do you think it is fitting that Connor has taken over the Graveyard?

Connor is probably the most suitable leader of Graveyard.

\1. He owns a good reputation and authority— from the “rumor” of Akron Unwind to what happens at Happy Jack Harvest Camp, the Unwinds respect and believe in him.

\2. He owns excellent leadership. Connor unites all the Unwinds in Graveyard to contribute to what they are fighting for.

\3. Connor can now handle this responsibility. The courage of risking for life remains while the impulsiveness for acting without thinking vanishes.

\4. Connor’s previous experiences are closely related to this place. He knows it well.

u Is the final message of the novel a positive one? Why?

It is a positive one, at least for Connor. Someone he loves, good friends, and all the kids facing the Unwind Order are all with him. Connor knows that now he doesn’t need to fight alone anymore. More importantly, he can see hope despite the threat of being exposed, which offers courage for all the Unwinds to live on.

Character Study for Pretty Girl and Plain Girl

UTA HAGEN

Questions for Character Study

  1. Who am I?

I am a teenage girl who is typically plain. When facing others’ teases, I stay humble and melancholy but still carefully tries to be myself— to do what other girls may do to make myself pretty.

  1. Where am I?

By the registration desk for the Galaxy Girl Pageant with chairs nearby.

  1. What surrounds me?

The registration desk, the registration paper, a pen, Tara, a bunch of chairs

  1. What time is it?

School time.

  1. What are my circumstances?

I want to register for the Galaxy Girl Pageant… I don’t know whether I’m overconfident or blind… (when I put on those heels at home, I felt a little bit exhilarated— I’m acting like a princess! It wouldn’t be too bad compared with other girls…) Although still timid and humble, I light up my courage, pretending to be perky, to say “yes” and register. However, Tara points out my white feather deep down and sharply. In great sadness, Tara keeps convincing me to drop out… but I believe I can stand any of the teases and insults. Maybe just I believe… I haven’t given up yet thanks to plenty of practices done which makes me feel invincible. So weird Tara is! Her confusing actions provoke my hidden sense of inferiority— yeah, the pretties are with the pretties and I’m do hurt by what Selene said. All the trials out of the comfort zone, like registering the pageant, is aiming to get myself out of the labels— “plain”, “boring”, “not worthy hanging out with”. I’m not giving up. I want to prove that people like Selene are wrong. But Tara’s passionate comfort pulls me out of the hidden fury and bigoted decisions. She makes her sacrifice outrightly, and eventually we come close to each other.

  1. What are my relationships?

Tara is my best friend and I found Selene distant who makes me feel humble and worthless.

  1. What do I want?

I want to register the Galaxy Girl Pageant to show that I’m also pretty compared with anyone else.

More importantly, deep down I want to rebuttal the people who define me as “boring” and “plain”.

  1. What is my obstacle?

\1. Tara teases me for my plain appearance

\2. The sense of inferiority due to other people e.g. Selene’s judgement.

  1. What do I do to get what I want?

I light up my courage to get out of my comfort zone. Register the Pageant and practice wearing heels a lot… what I am doing is aiming to be myself rather than remaining intimidated by the insults.

Tried-and-true Love (final version)

Erich Fromm, a German social psychologist, argues that “love makes men overcome the sense of isolation and separateness, yet it permits to be himself, to remain his integrity.” In the dystopian novel Unwind, a lovely pair emerges— Risa and Connor. Their life traces overlap once the Unwind Orders are signed: they both go into exile. However, Risa and Connor seem to come from two planets more often than not— “isolation” and “separateness” are conspicuous. So, is it only their common goal of staying alive that binds them? Are Planet Connor and Planet Risa connected? Through thorough reading, it is not hard to register that the affection between Risa and Connor springs up owing to both their similarities and differences.

One of Risa and Connor’s “few” similarities is the perfect interpretation of the Admiral’s supreme rules—especially for “rise to the occasion”. The duo head for a bus stop tacitly at the instant when a police car appears, which indicates that they have been “molded” into coherent “underground workers” who keep “running will attract attention” in mind, and meanwhile flexible enough to utilize surroundings as a cover— high-school-aged kids. Comparatively, the couple’s escaping plan is also coolly probative. Connor makes a split-second decision of pushing back cops, followed by rushing onto the interstate. Risa does the same except for the details— her destination is a forest and there’s no one blocking her due to the car accident caused by Connor. In these cases, they are both holding wild intension towards surviving, so even though the chance comes in a hurry that could simultaneously wither away, they will succeed to grab it. Clearly, Risa and Connor are naturals in finding out the first priority, which makes them “excellent” AWOLs. This facilitates the first step of love—mutual appreciation.

It is also very explicit to notice that the lovers have grown fearless. Back to the days at State Home, Risa shuddered when being order to “satisfy” budget cuts, almost involuntarily asking for other professions. Similarly, Connor needed Ariana to light up his courage to face Unwind Order. they were too intimidated by the prospect compared with what happens on them later: Risa becomes a pianist in Happy Jack Harvest Camp while Connor ultimately walks into the unwinding room safe and sound. As the old saying goes, “what doesn’t kill you makes you stronger.” Having gone through so many life and death tests, now they take it easy. At least they have tried, leaving no regret. Risa and Connor stand the test of time so a solid bond is formed between each other. Now they are truly ready for love.

However, when it comes to the differences, will the emerging love shatters?

Well, they own distinct characteristics. It is like a battle between ice and fire when referring to “rational versus emotional”. Connor constantly causes trouble as getting into a bunch of fights at school, and adding a “troublesome” new-born to their exile. Conversely his girlfriend is able to put her hatred aside while remaining observant towards Roland—even after the “Bathroom Event”. She is also sober enough to scan the danger of taking that new-born baby and then try her best to “wake” Connor up. It seems like whether diverse emotions can be effectively controlled or not is the key difference between the couple, and their personality traits are just complementary. Nevertheless, their characteristics gradually interweave afterwards— Risa begins to care about Connor instead of keeping distances, and Connor can amazingly control his fury. As they comprehending and then coming close to each other, yet still “remain integrity”, the seed of love sprouts.

Love doesn’t mean “identical”. The similarities between couples can only get them qualified and prepared for love. Tried-and-true love will appear only if both sides build a bridge over any existing gap, since that demonstrates great efforts for love, rather than illusion of romance. Between Risa and Connor love obtains the sublimation and the eternal.
