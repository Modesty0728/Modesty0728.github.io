Annotated Bibliography for Peer Pressure

the American Academy of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry. “Peer Pressure.” Merriam Webster, March 2018, https://www.aacap.org/AACAP/Families_and_Youth/Facts_for_Families/FFF-Guide/Peer-Pressure-104.aspx.Accessed 24 November, 2020.

Our project work is related to peer pressure and I’m searching for information on the internet to learn this phenomenon better. This valuable website shows a clear definition of peer pressure and provides for categorized suggestions in terms of different groups of people.

Peer pressure is a feeling that one must act same as people in one’s age and social group in order to be regarded and respected. It is natural and beneficial for children to interact with friends as they grow and mature and actually peers play a vital role in the social and emotional development of teenagers. Their influences which can be either positive or supportive begin at an early age and increase through the teenage years. For the good aspects, peers are helpful in fostering one’s new skills and interests in numerous social and daily activities and events. On contrary, with the same strong level of effect, peers can also egg their companions on delinquencies and even risky behaviors, e.g. skipping classes, theft, cheating, remaining addicted to drugs or alcohol, etc. Due to the results of peer pressure, the majority of teens with substance abuse would begin using drugs of alcohols. This terrifying pressure can appear among both real life and social media. However, minors are always in concern of being left out or made fun by peers so would go along with the group.

There are still some tips on dealing with peer pressure. For teenagers, they need to stay away from “dangerous” peers and learn to say “no”. Meanwhile it’s very good for them to get in touch with other peers who resist peer pressure and talk to a reliable adult. When it comes to the parents, they are responsible to communicate with teenagers honestly and teach them the ways to resist. The friends and teachers of teens can be a useful target to share concerns with. Also, parents ought to prepare the backups in case of unpredictable situations for them. Last but not least, if the grown-ups manage to convert their children into the assertive ones, less likely such pressure would be gained.

All the information above is vital to my research. First of all, a clear definition is demonstrated so that it’s easier to understand the problems we are facing and the factors behind. Moreover, the advice is adaptable for the consideration of all groups of people who are able to improve the situation. In this way, I can draw conclusions of the available methods to solve peer pressure where our main subject is exactly focusing.

You Are Everything

You Are Everything

The earth was born in chaotic dark.

Final silence thawed, dripping into night,

into the Milky Way fleeing out like the sword of knight,

piercing the noisy solemnity,

to the birth of words of humanity.

You are willing to render

all the cute creatures and colorful features,

with arms around me like a border.

You keep me company in fear.

Without you, the world will be lackluster.

You are supplying us with snug underneath the skin,

like the air I breathe I let you in.

I’m giving in to your touch but will never get enough.

You keep me keen in Spring green.

Without you, the world will be loveless and tough.

You are the submarines,

diving deep into the sea with marines.

Into the water, into a culture,

crystal blue silk is flowing and a brand-new world is coming.

Ebulliently you hearten marines to roam,

enjoying a feast of knowledge as if they are at home.

You keep me curious into the unknown rather than frightened.

Without you, the world will be unenlightened.

I praise your great animation,

and the intangible contribution,

which created diverse nations.

And I’m so proud of you,

dedicating varied points of view.

You are the origin of creatures,

the continuity of cultures.

You are also our mother,

the sweet stream moistening sandy desert.

John A Birth of a country

Compared to what I learned before, this film’s depiction of issues in Canada was more specific. From other group’s “deadlock” presentation, I got to know there were clashes among different parties, however the film demonstrated the stiff situations in terms of loads of debating and cliques which helped more in understanding.

It also changed my perspective towards the process leading to Confederation. Before watching I believed the evolution of Government was smooth-going. But it turned out to be a tortuous process since Cabinet stepped down several times before the coalition and the leaders would change their allies based on interests.

To solve the headache of political deadlock and American threat, George Brown, from Clear Grit Party, abandoned the proposal of separating the colonies and allied with leaders of Liberal-Conservative Party and Le Parti Bleus, namely Macdonald and Cartier in order to create possibilities for double majority which enabled Government to function and pass laws to build defense against the US.

Overall, the main theme was the essential passion for the birth of a country. In the beginning, politicians made judgements depending on proposals of own parties rather considering the colonies as a whole, e.g. Brown disapproved the construction of railway as he thought this way to achieve modernization was a theft of the public purse. Gradually, due to the threat of the USA and the concern of losing the very nation, Brown suggested acting together to reform the constitution. Spontaneously Confederation was achieved in the end to protect the common homeland. In other words, the film showed stronger and stronger identity of Canadiens.

I felt exhilarated to the film because of the great idea of Confederation. When Macdonald came up with this idea and Brown chose to stand by him, their faces shined in the sunlight. Those two scenes were the climax, heartening and indicating the coming of a historical moment. Naturally I perceived a historic trend of unity both in the film and Chinese history, e.g. CPC fought against Japanese with KMT which protected China’s dominion. Also, as unity is strength, a bundle of sticks can be hardly broken despite I’ve tried my best. The film really resonated with me.

Craven in *The Raven*?

Edgar Allan Poe once said, “We loved with a love that was more than love.” It seems like sometimes “love” is just a word for expressing rather than a true deep feeling even people themselves are not conscious of. In one of Poe’s most famous poem, The Raven, he wrote about an “absurd” “communication with a raven in a gothic style, giving vent to the melancholy for the loss of love nearly in his delirium. Absolutely this indicates the purpose of this piece of work is more than “the raven”, and the “intangible love” can be exactly reflected by the theme: seeking hope in despair is struggling.

Sometimes the theme is also “intangible”. The speaker could no longer distinguish between illusion and reality. Pathetically, he thinks the raven is sent by the angles to help forget pain (Poe, lines 87-89). The poor man is so eager to meet the love again that the extreme melancholy makes him hallucinate. He does not recognize it as a ridiculous thought about imagination. But it is crucial to notice that the illusion is a way of “seeking hope” for the speaker. Perhaps it sounds romantic, but behind it is the hysterical screaming in heart.

One of the objective factors leading to despair is the cruel reality closely related to hard life of the speaker. In the last stanza, the melancholy soul is trapped in the raven’s shadow forever (Poe, lines 107-108). “Nevermore” is repeated and emphasizing the eternal loss of the love Lenore several times. The speaker’s communication with the raven is more like a talking to another part of himself. The raven symbolizes the great loss in heart so the shadow of the raven is actually reflecting speaker’s intense pain. Maybe readers do not know how torturous it is, but the speaker has suggested that he would not get rid of the sorrow anymore by using the word “trapped”. Pathetically the dead cannot get back and the speaker is in such a deep love. The cold and heartless actuality makes speaker a decadent who is even tired of driving the raven away and cheering himself up.

Looking for solace is another form of “seeking hope”. The speaker surceases the disconsolate emotion through reading (Poe, line 10). There’s no doubt that he tries to numb himself through distractions and utilize the discourse to release sadness. Also, even though no one is outside the door, the speaker keeps whispering “Lenore!”, demonstrating the desire to meet the love again (Poe, lines 28-29). The wish is also a piece of comfort the speaker wants. For the speaker, calming himself down is hard, hence “seeking help” is about the same.

The theme, about “struggling”, can be proved through exploring its causes and extra meaning in The Raven. The speaker’s struggle seems lamentable in the pain of the loss of Lenore, but he is also showing a tenacious spirit. The past is in the past, and mortals’ power is impossible to change history. Maybe they are stuck into sorrow inevitably, but the struggle for hope is always meaningful which proves the meaning of their existence, the love.

Should Children under 13 Be Allowed on Social Network Sites?

Do a thirteen years old child have an effective self-control？The answer is no. Imagine it, even an adult has difficulty of reducing time on the internet, how can a child be success? What is of great importance is that the “difficulty” can generally lead to children’s getting negative information from the internet. To be specific, children under 13 should not be allowed on social network considering their mental health, and time management.

Varieties of information on social networks can silently addict children, thus the online stuff keeps them away from reality which causes mental isolation. According to a survey carried out by Benedick University and Proston College in 2014, children feel more anxious and stressful during face-to-face communication after using the social network to interact with others. Nowadays minors use apps like Facebook to contact with friends or family due to the development of electronic technology and the social conduct of pro-online. As they gradually get used to share thoughts online which avoids stress caused by oral communication, relying on this safety zone without embarrassing themselves. Nevertheless the loneliness in terms of mental isolation prevents children from expression which is phenomenal for exploring creativity, thus minors remain rigescent.

Social networks can also disrupt children’s time management. Spontaneously, their daily schedule change, damaging the physical health. Since too much time is spent on “virtual wonderland”, kids fail to be organized and then come to the disruption of internal clock representing the unregular resting time. According to a recent research about the status-in-quo of children’s use of network, the number of five-year-old minors who spends over thirty minutes in browsing internet has already achieved a half. As kids grow older, the time occupied by devices would only escalate. Pathetically, the physical health of young ages is essential for the whole life’s well-being as a matter of fact. In other words, the addiction on the internet could have a profound implication on children, damaging their well-being forwards. For instance, staying up late leads to a decline on intelligence.

All in all, due to the concern of children’s mental and physical health, the date for going online ought to be delayed, assumed as a necessary protection against the distemper prospect. These two factors are the most fundamental ones categorized from obvious facts e.g. dreadful time-organization. To deal with such issues, great effort needs offering to guarantee minors with a thriving future for they are the world’s “future”, the hope every person depended on.

What Was the Main Cause of the 1837 Rebellions?

In 1837, violent rebellions broke out in both Lower Canada and Upper Canada which were British North American colonies. The rebels in Lower Canada were led by Papineau and Nelson from the Parti Canadien to overthrow Governor Lord Gosford who was supported by the Chateau Clique. In Upper Canada, the rebels were led by Mackenzie, who was a radical reformer against Lieutenant Governor Francis Bond Head and his supporters from the Tories (Family Compact). There were many causes related to economic, political and cultural issues. I believe that the most important was political factors.

The main reason for the rebellion was the way the colony was governed. All key positions were in the hands of people that were appointed and they were mostly from a British background. The Executive and Legislative Councils and the Governors could veto any proposals from Legislative Assembly so the government was not responsible or representative. Governor Gosford only passed 10 resolutions out of 92 which angered Papineau. They threatened to not pass money bills so the British said they would help themselves to treasury, angering the Legislative Assembly thereby Nelson would call the rebel to arms. Therefore the main cause of the rebellions was the government being ineffective and not listening to the people so they took matters into their own hands and started a rebellion.

Another reason were the issues of the Governors appointed by English monarch. They were unfamiliar with local problems and conditions so they depended on the advice of councils. Sir Francis Bond Head ignored the two leading Reformers’ suggestions. Soon the Legislative Assembly blocked money bills because of this. As a result, all work on certain positions like docks and roads came to halt immediately. Obviously this led to an unrest in Upper Canada which later turned to be the rebellion of Mackenzie.

Besides political reasons, the economic factors were also vital. One of these factors was the land issue. Best land was given to Family Compact while most of land was controlled by Executive Legislative Council (90%) and Anglican Church (1/7). As more and more settlers came in, they could only get poor and uncleared farmland. Then in 1836, the habitants experienced a crop failure thus they suffered from starvation in the next year. In the end, a rebellion was organized as a result of being starved.

Transportation issue also mattered the economy. The merchants were eager to build canals and roads to achieve modernization so loads of taxes were collected by the Government. Farmers also needed roads to get to markets. However, as merchants grew rich, farmers felt frustrated because government did not grant enough land and money to them. The depressed emotion among farmers eventually caused the rebellion full of anger.

We also should be reminded of the social and cultural factors. There were special privileges for a small group of people. All the officials were appointed by Governor, such as councils, judges, sheriffs, etc. What’s more, in Upper Canada, good occupations are granted to Family Compact and friends. As the government was not meritocratic at all, the gap between the rich and poor was widening further. In the depression of 1837, the Canadiens attacked the government because people with special privileges were too rich. The privileges finally led to the flames of fury of the public, spontaneously, people came into conflict.

In conclusion, the tension in Upper and Lower Canada from 1791 to 1837 was caused due to the economic, political, and cultural or social issues appeared in the temporal society, among which the political factors were the most predominant. Amounts of the economic and social and phenomenon demonstrated was based on political decisions which were the radical causes of the rebellions.

Works Cited Page

P. Clark. Canada Revisited. Arnold Pub Ltd. Jun 01, 1993

Equality vs Fairness

All men are created equal, or so reckoned Thomas Jefferson as he drafted American’s Declaration of Independence in 1776. “Equality” has been a vital topic for a long time. By comparison, the situation is the same to “Fairness”. But are the two terms actually identical? In the story Harrison Bergeron, introducing a dystopia society in which the government is too crazy about egalitarianism, the difference among them emerges. The definitions are distinct for fairness is an uncertain process full of emotions and equality, however, represents something people are born with but then still be defined as a life destination. To be more specific, the difference between equality and fairness is related to the subjectivity, quality, and possibility.

As a matter of fact, fairness is much more humanized than equality, which is more subjectivity. The main character of this story, Harrison, wears extremely heavy handicaps, earphones, and spectacles (Vonnegut 3). It is definitely a miserable experience that everyone tries to get away from. To achieve absolute equality, the governor puts little attention on preserving the human liberty of enjoying advantages, and even destroys it. On the contrary, Harrison dances with his empress freely after getting rid of the handicaps. Real fairness needs protecting personal legal rights. It is exactly what Harrison wanted and achieved in the end. Clearly fairness is respectable to human being thus everyone is truly chirrupy. In other words, fairness is reasonable and can better release human nature than equality.

When it comes to the quality, sometimes, people cannot realize the fact that fairness is a process while equality stands for a result. The handicaps and masks for the characters are different methods to ensure everyone’s ability is at the same level. “Fairness” is taken into every government act in this controlling process, preventing the privileges like encouraging ugly masks to the beauties. “Fairness” is a tool in their daily life to achieve the final equality. After so much effort put in, eventually society in the US is “equal” for fairness has developed along the right lines. This phenomenon remains considered as a “satisfied” result. By analyzing the logic and evidence, it turns out to be a truth that fairness is a way of struggling to be equal. They play individual roles in different positions or stages.

Jefferson emphasized “All men are created equal”, somehow fairness exists uncertainly when babies are born in some cases. Obviously, in Harrison Bergeron, everybody leads a mortal life in which they enjoy the rights to do personal things, normal and basic, like watching TV and marrying someone. Basically it just goes to show the equal rights people have. Then turning to George, Harrison’s father. His intelligence is way above normal but the one of his wife’s is pretty normal. Disabilities among babies would certainly condemn them to a life of panic and frustration, but these situations are inevitable. Individualism and differences are always there. In conclusion, equality is supposed to describe the rights everyone has. The rights are given without disregarding anyone, but carrying unfair qualities with them possibly.

All in all, the difference consists of three indispensable parts. Admittedly, it seems that fairness and equality are similar although fairness is more subjective and colorful. We can fight for this and considering it as a faith. Then without plenty of concerns, equality would come in the end itself.

Viridescent Hyacinth

“Remember to wear more clothes, sweetheart.”

“Mommy! Of course. Take care!”

The woman was waving at her daughter, watching the train leave the station. A clammy wind blew up, freezing me up instantly. I lay on the bench, envious and lonely. Two months ago, my parents were dead in a big fire. This happened so rapidly that the abrupt pain worked like alcohol, numbing my nerves. No relatives and property were left except a few clothes. The train station was considered as my temporary shelter. I began to beg for food two weeks ago, however giving it up because of burnout. Currently, I was thinking about how to get over the starvation as I would never give up my life facilely. There were bound to be some methods of getting food money without begging.

I had a flash of inspiration.

No, it was vicious, definitely.

For the next hour, World War III broke out in my brain. The crazy idea came into my mind time after time, frustrating me. It was a moral violation! Mom always said I was a virtuous girl. Her words lingered in my ears, a long time cannot be dispersed. But nothing was left for me now, besides I would return it back in the end… Then I took out my cellphone slowly.

I was sorry, nonetheless I wanted to survive.

Taking a deep breath, I dialed a casual number.

“Du…”my cellphone began to warn like an alarm. My heart beat violently, as if all the surroundings were staring at me, preparing to expose my crime.

A voice interrupted the terrifying atmosphere.

“Who’s there?”

That voice must be a lady’s.

“Umm, I—”
“Hang on a second. I told you I cannot afford these cosmetics! Please get out .”

I stopped immediately. How could I cheat the poor lady for money? But before I hang up the phone, she continued—

“Forgive me, sometimes salesmen can be extremely annoying you know. So, what do you want?”

“Umm, madam,” I whispered, “Your child is my friend—”

“You mean Hyacinth?”

“S…sorry, would you mind speaking a little bit louder?”

“I mean, Hyacinth, you definitely know that! What a perfect name! In fact viridescent hyacinths represent kindness…”

“Oh—exactly! Your daughter, that’s right, she is my friend, and—is she at home now?”

Suddenly the lady fell silent.

“No. She is not here.”

“Madam, Hyacinth is in trouble now and is badly in need of financial help!” I had tried my best to shout out anxiously and was just about to explain the trouble more specifically, to my surprise, the lady replied without doubting.

“She needs money, right?”

“Yes.” I mustered the courage to make myself sound steadfast.

Another silence seemed like the peace before the erupting of a volcano.

“I will definitely give enough money to people in need.”

“I convey my appreciation to you for your daughter!” Despite I am the person in need coincidently, I felt ashamed.

“You know,” suddenly said the lady, “You must be a girl with a kind heart, for you turn to me for help instantly when my daughter met difficulty.”

“Not really…”

“Whatever you say, but I’d like to consider you, the friend of Hyacinth, as my own daughter.”

I felt a familiar warmth inside, so real that nearly made me cry out.

“It’s just a duty to care about minors.”

My nose stung. Tears stood in my dull eyes.

“If you accept, let me be your second mother, protecting you till the end.”

I could not control my feeling anymore. All the lies crumbled—

“Madam! I’m sorry. What I said were all made up to cheat you for money! I ought to be put into prison, but I just want to be alive! You are the only person being nice to me after my parents’ deaths. I’m a bad girl humiliating your kindness. I will go to the police myself.”

“My dear child,” The lady chuckled, “This is what I’m waiting for. You have admitted your shame. For me, that’s enough. Don’t define yourself as a fraud, ever, because the ‘wicked’ idea has been defeated by your conscience.”

Her words melted my ice-cold heart. In place of tears, there was the beginning of a smile. “I… appreciate and admire you… more than I can say. Can I send my respects to your daughter as well?”

“She won’t be back.”

“How—how can it be?”

“She died three days ago.”

Another silence, longer than the shut-eye of Sleeping Beauty.

“My condolences.”

“That’s ok.”

“You’ve identified my true purpose in the beginning, haven’t you?”

“Yep. I was trying to give you a helping hand in life, and leading you to the right track, if possible. You are guilty, but I’m sincere. I’ve considered you as my second daughter indeed.”

I could say nothing but letting tears flow freely.

“Don’t forget you’ve got a new home. It’s getting colder and colder outside. Remember to wear more clothes, sweetheart.”

“Of course. Mommy.”

Psalm 25 says, “Good and upright is the LORD: therefore will he teach sinners in the way.” Nothing can beat kindness, for it has magic to keep you away from evil. Above all, kindness resonates among us. A prosperous and steady society depends on nothing but kindness. I’ve got a new home, sweet and cozy. Thank god, someone appeared in my despair, beautiful as viridescent hyacinths.
