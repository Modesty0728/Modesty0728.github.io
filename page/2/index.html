Do You Think Today’s Health and Beauty Industry is Dangerous?

It depends. People from distinct religions and regions are supporting heterogeneous beauty standards. The same fact is—people are always chasing for beauty, because nearly everyone wants to have a not-so-bad look. And that is normal, very common based on human nature. So, if we only consider the perfect beauty standards, the health and beauty industry are safe, since these represent people’s nature of admiring beauty and their ideal figures. However, what we are concerning now is the methods used to approach these standards. Friends around me would instinctively laugh at my relevantly darker skin, frequently going on diets, and even plan carefully for a future plastic surgery. I’ve always been confused. Instinctively I cannot help but disapproving their manners. But why? I mean—everyone has their rights to chase for beauty, so is this also their right to take any extreme ways to achieve their goals? Maybe it is. So why am I denying their legitimate decisions? Perhaps it is due to my instinct to stick up for “natural beauty”. I even get uncomfortable when seeing my friends infatuated with make-ups. I’ve reflected on myself for so many times. Don’t be so extreme! Don’t be so harsh on your friends if yourself do not like it! You have to understand it is others’ right! Besides, even myself once planned to purchase a bunch of Dior or Channel’s cosmetics when growing into an adolescent. Literally, I enjoyed considering about that kind of thing! That made me feel confident, as if my life was extremely colorful. I can only tell that like me, preferring nature can refer to less care in personal appearance (well I still make sure that I’m in a tidy and easeful look). It is really hard for me to answer whether striving to be pretty by using whatever possible methods is correct and rational. I can only say that there is sequential safe level for health and beauty industry. The more thoughts you put on this field, the more “risks” you are likely to take since you’ve put too much precious energy on superficial fantasy. Deep down, infinite emptiness is out of ignorance because you are not living for yourself anymore but your appearance.

Fairy Godmother (script analysis unit)

I am a supporting character in Disney’s story Cinderella, the physical embodiment of hope. I am Cinderella’s godmother. I always appear suddenly in the thin air with twinkling lights (sparkly confetti), wearing a gown with two wings shaking behind her back and a wand (can be summoned from nothingness) at the end of which is grafted a shiny tin foil star. I’m so warm-hearted that I could not bear any unfair. So, I offer poor Cinderella a little bit of help…make her princess dream come true: a dress and a tiara. In fairy tales, I’m “a fairy with magical powers who acts as a mentor or parent to someone, in the role that an actual godparent was expected to play in many societies.” I stand for children’s fantasies about magic, and “whenever events are right for a fairy tale to recur, I steps in to make sure that the tale in question runs its course with as few fatalities as possible.” I wouldn’t appear to Ella if she loses her faith. In the evening. After Cinderella’s abusers left. Before the ball gets started.

I’m equipped with a magical wand. I was appointed be to Cinderella’s fairy godmother. I am summoned by Cinderella since she hasn’t lost her faith even when she is at her weakest point.

I’m Cinderella’s godmother. As a fairy godmother, I am also children’s beacon of hope.

I want Cinderella to go to the ball. I want Cinderella happy. I want Cinderella to always keep her faith.

My precious wand can also be used by bad people. When magic is used as dark force, I would probably lose my job!! And the whole world would get messed up. Revolution of the Ocean (final version)

Darwin started studying artificial selection as early as the nineteenth century, feeding and experimenting on pigeons which were selected according to individual traits (Hooper, 2017). Obviously, human beings have been exploring this field for centuries and their curiosity continues. After adapting to the environment, normally the advantageous living beings would be chosen to provide people with a more satisfying life. The genetically modified organisms, also known as GMOs, are one of the profound products or examples of artificial selection of which “genetic materials are artificially manipulated” according to the definition of GMO. In other words, GMOs are created “against nature”. Our research focuses on the first consuming-allowed bioengineered animal ever—GM salmon (also known as AquAdvantage salmon) created by AquaBounty Technologies (Nordrum, 2020).

This biotechnology enlarges salmon’s size (comparatively a doubled size in a certain growing period), and the time they take to grow to market size is halved compared with conventional Atlantic salmon which are definitely slow growers with a 32 to 36 months’ life circle approximately (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.).

So far, only a few countries have been using this method to improve salmon’s quality, e.g. US, Canada, and Argentina. The first transgenic salmon came down in pike in 1989, Canada, which could grow all year around rather than just in spring and summer (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.). On November 19th, 2015, with food and environmental safety both ensured, AquaBounty’s genetically modified salmon came into season with the approval of the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA). People are gradually accepting genetically modified salmon.

So, what are the details about this “delicious” technology? AquAdvantage salmon are originally Atlantic salmon inserted another two special genes from the chinook salmon (Oncorhynchus tshawytscha) and Ocean Pout. Generally, the scientists transfer the growth hormone (or growth promoting gene) into fertilized Atlantic salmon eggs. Therefore, the original (Atlantic) ones manage to grow faster in warmer seawater. All ultimate products are sterile females (The Fish Site, 2010), which directly diminishes the concern of them breeding with wild salmon. Wenonah Hauter, the chairman of Food and Water Watch, also came up with disapprovals based on salmon’s wild competition ability. Once again, it has been backfired by Dr. Eric Hallerman who claims that GM salmon are domesticated (The Fish Site, 2010). Current results also indicate that AquAdvantage salmon are less competitive than their “ancestors” because of the low quantity requirement of food resources. However, although it finally became true that FDA approved the GM salmon, about 60 retailers (combined with 4,660 stores) refuse to have GM salmon sold in their stores (Shreeves, 2019) as GMO labels are never required on AquaBounty Salmon—consumers could be unaware of the particularity of what they have just eaten. It is also being said that AquAdvantage salmon contain less nutrient—“producing less of the beneficial omega—3 fatty acids than non-GMO salmon” (Say NO to GMO Salmon + Full-Length Feature Online: Salmon Confidential, 2013). There are worries based on the survival of wild animals and human health indeed, but undeniably AquAdvantage salmon benefit the environment. For instance, they actually improve the conservation of wild fish populations. As aforementioned, the demand for food decreases. That sustains the aquaculture effectively. Moreover, where GM salmon are bred is very close to the grocery store, which lowers carbon emissions during transportation. AquaBounty is also perfectly capable of recycling waste water and resources (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.).

“GMOs” has always been a controversial topic. In science class, we talked about its pros and cons. One of the biggest concerns is about GMO’s uncertainty in the future. Through this meaningful research, we see how amazingly a new genetic product came out, and how scientists argue, raising up questions, analyzing, researching…We can only say that up to now the AquAdvantage salmon are relatively friendly to the earth. No matter what would happen on this brand-new species, we should always appreciate and continue the great efforts put in this field which promote the development of genetics.

Generally, the scientists transfer the growth hormone (or growth promoting gene) into fertilized Atlantic salmon eggs. Therefore, the original (Atlantic) salmon manage to grow faster in warmer seawater. All ultimate products are sterile females (The Fish Site, 2010), which directly diminishes the concern of them breeding with wild salmon. Wenonah Hauter, the chairman of Food and Water Watch, also came up with disapprovals based on salmon’s wild competition ability. Once again, it has been backfired by Dr. Eric Hallerman who claims that GM salmon are domesticated (The Fish Site, 2010). Current results also indicate that AquAdvantage salmon are less competitive than their “ancestors” because of the low quantity requirement of food resources. However, although it finally became true that FDA approved the GM salmon, about 60 retailers (combined with 4,660 stores) refuse to have GM salmon sold in their stores (Shreeves, 2019) as GMO labels are never required on AquaBounty Salmon—consumers could be unaware of the particularity of what they have just eaten. It is also being said that AquAdvantage salmon contain less nutrient—“producing less of the beneficial omega—3 fatty acids than non-GMO salmon” (Say NO to GMO Salmon + Full-Length Feature Online: Salmon Confidential, 2013). There are worries based on the survival of wild animals and human health indeed, but undeniably AquAdvantage salmon benefit the environment. For instance, they actually improve the conservation of wild fish populations. As aforementioned, the demand for food decreases. That sustains the aquaculture effectively. Moreover, where GM salmon are bred is very close to the grocery store, which lowers carbon emissions during transportation. AquaBounty also has the ability to recycle waste water and resources (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.).

“GMOs” has always been a controversial topic. In science class, we talked about its pros and cons. One of the biggest concerns is about GMO’s uncertainty in the future. Through this meaningful research, we see how amazingly a new genetic product came out, and how scientists argue, raising up questions, analyzing, researching…We can only say that up to now the AquAdvantage salmon are relatively friendly to the earth. No matter what would happen on this brand-new species, we should always appreciate and continue the great efforts put in this field which promote the development of genetics.
