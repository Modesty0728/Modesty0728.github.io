unwind part3 questions

Part THREE:

u Why does Hayden call the harvest camps “black holes”? Is the metaphor an appropriate one? (pg 80)

\1. A black hole is a region of spacetime where nothing—no particles or even electromagnetic radiation such as light—can escape from it. According to Hayden, no one knows what happens in the harvest camp only the final result of being unwound. In other words, no one exactly knows the process of unwinding and the treatments on unwinds.

\2. The metaphor is quite appropriate because it clearly tells us how mysterious the harvest camp and the unwinding process is and also emphasizes that this unknown place is some kind of horrible, connecting to this novel’s writing topic—dystopia.

u Who is Humphrey Dunfree? Do you think his story is truth, fiction, or something in-between? (pgs 81-82)

\1. He is the “survivor” of unwind order. Like other kids, Humphrey get what he “deserved” after his parents sign the order who however, going mad towards it. Then they hacked in to The National Unwind Database, getting to know each Humphrey’s single part’s new owners and secretly searching them to paying what they owed to Humphrey back in the way of rearrangement.

\2. I think his story is something between truth and fiction. According to the previous context, a computer virus diminished Connor’s discussion towards Humphrey. I don’t think it is a coincidence or the government wouldn’t be so stressed out and furious. However, Humphrey’s story is also too uncanny to believe so I suppose some parts of it is made up by imagination or exaggeration, but it must be based on the real event. No matter the story is one-hundred percent realistic or not, it’s quite sure that in this mad world parents’ guilty emotions exist and there are some crazy actions to take trying to turn the unwound result into reversible. It clearly shows the fragility of human nature and people are struggling against the dystopia.

u Why does Sonia have the Unwinds write letters home? What does Connor learn from his letter writing experience? (pg 85)

\1. Sonia wants the unwinds to write down everything not being said before to someone they love. And the letters will be sent once they reached eighteen.

(a. For the unwinds,

is a good chance for releasing themselves from the miserable memories. Writing letters also helps them recognize themselves’ true attitudes towards everything, including family, unwind order, and the crazy world. No matter what would happen in the next, at least the unwinds take up the responsibility of speaking up for themselves and the people who are suffering in dystopia. The unwinds have already made a contribution on behalf of themselves to the world. Sonia makes the unwinds’ “useless” life meaningful.

Self-writing also helps them relieved with no regret left.

(b. For their guardians,

No matter their kids would survive or not, the guardians would receive the letter on the unwinds’ eighteenth birthday. Sonia chooses this special day for certain reasons. It is like the guardians push their kids into water without looking back, and one day they see the same pool however with no water and simultaneously are sent a previous video about how the stifling kids were struggling beneath water without knowing whether the poor kids survive or not. When guardians receive letter, there are not danger for the unwinds anymore. However, the easeful status-quo would definitely get them realized how gloomy the hell that they have put kids into is. As aforementioned, it’s just like watching kids suffering from over head water when there’s no water remains anymore. Objectively, Sonia aims to get the guardians at least been aware of how miserable the kids are under the unwind order. And subjectively, she wants the guardians to respect their kids’ thoughts by reading letters and reflect on themselves about their recognition towards human nature. By letting the guardians know the unwinds better, there’s a slight hope that they may also contribute a voice to the world by changing the attitude towards “unwind” to gradually make less kids suffer. After all, they the ones who decide life and death to some extent.

\2. From the writing experience, Connor recognizes many things.

(a. Firstly, He doesn’t truly love Ariana because she is not the one offering hope and strength now, and that’s why Connor thinks writing to Ariana is pointless.

(b. Deep down in Connor’s heart, he still loves his parents. Connor’s sweet memories towards his family seem to be blocked once the unwind order is found. Everything his family do next is shaded with darkness which looks so hopeless that Connor’s heart begins to be full of it, which numbs his mind and makes him feel like all the memories is in darkness now. In other words, it is proved to be an illusion and the precious deep love Connor’s parents offered is still too strong and impressed to forget. That’s why Connor finds himself still love them, as a spontaneous respond to their love.

u What is the analogy Risa makes between Connor and the teacher/Hannah? How does Connor respond to the question of “Do you want it?” (pg 88)

\1. Hannah is willing to take the baby, however Connor asks whether Hannah wants the baby, and then Risa wants Connor’s answer towards the question if it is set for him.

\2. Connor hesitates for a while and goes on the truck after emphasizing the baby’s gender—female. Deep down Connors knows that Hannah and him is the same. They don’t want the baby, but are willing to take it and protect it from danger. Now Connor has finished his responsibility and willingness of take care about the baby to be less guilty towards previous experience. He knows he has done his best to save her, and that’s enough.

u What’s the difference between a run-away and a run-to? Why do you think CyFi is a run-to? (pg 92)

\1. A run-to has a destination is his/her mind, which is the main difference compared with a run-away. Run-aways and run-toes are all escaping from the terrible unwind order, however the latter has a more explicit plan.

\2. We all know that in the previous context, Risa, Connor and Lev are exactly three run-aways who are always nervous and wary to deal with occasional emergencies. CyFi is completely different because he is quite relaxed walking along railway and knows exactly where he is going to although he cannot tell.

u Why does Lev feel like he’s an accomplice to murder? Why is this word choice and connotation significant? (pg 94)

\1. Because Risa and Connor now are probably and caught and then unwound, which is all lev’s fault. Lev turned the two “kidnappers” in so he feels ashamed to push them to the end of their life.

\2. (a. Firstly this word shows Connor’s point of view is changing.

(c. This word is thought-provoking. It makes us think about the nature of unwinding again. Is it murdering or not? This word not only fosters lev to think deeply ignoring his family’s ideas, but also allows readers to have their own speculations because there’s no exact answer.

u How is CyFi’s experience with Unwind parts different from everyone else we’ve read about so far? How do you think this could cause problems? (pgs 95-96)

\1. CyFi was hit when riding a hike so his right temporal lobe is widely damaged. However, instead of getting body parts or bits e.g. an arm, CyFi got an entire temporal lobe during the surgeon.

\2. Scattered body parts may have some body memories, but are individual and weak. But an entire temporal lobe means part of the entire nerve system, and individual tissues are connecting into each other. That means CyFi got a real strong and independent lobe which can influence and even control him sometimes.

u What does Lev realize on page 99? How is this significant for his character?

\1. Lev realizes that CyFi is acting weirdly. There’s something in his eyes and voice. CyFi is now lagging behind Lev and looks like a shuffle, which indicates that he is quite nervous or stressful. The way of his talking is also converted from Old Umber into menacing.

\2. This shows that Lev is wary and observant.

1. Lev realizes that his parents love God more than loving him and he hates them for it. His parents always do what supposed to do but are too crazy about their faith to deeply love someone in the real life like Lev. Recognize the fact

\2. Lev’s recognition might be a turning point for his character. Before Pastor Dan told him to run away, he is brainwashed by the idea of the high God by his family. Lev then runs away just trying to be alone and reset thoughts. Through the calm and deep thinking after escaping from school, he finally finds out his hidden hatred to the cold love of family and is more empathetic than he used to be when being brainwashed. Lev’s recognition also demonstrates his beginning point of re-recognizing himself. To some extent, the change of Lev is also implying he would meet Risa and Connor again in the future, and at that time they all of the three are aiming to survive from unwind order along with the disappointment this world, based on which the author is trying to show how crazy dystopia is. Recognize himself

u Who stole the Christmas ornament? What does this tell us about Unwinding? (pg 102)

\1. The boy who lives as one-eighth CyFi’s brain stole the Christmas ornament. He controlled CyFi himself and acted like what he did before getting unwound.

\2. This tells the readers more about unwinding. Unwinding indeed has the potential danger to the one who got the body parts because the organs are not theirs technically. The potential danger is threatening, since people who benefit from unwinding can be mess up. More importantly, the potential danger makes unwinding a more negative thing, since it ruins the life of both kids under order and people who “get help”.

u Why is CyFi taking Lev to Joplin, Missouri? (pg 106)

Because the boy who lives as a part of CyFi wants to get there. CyFi is bothered by his thoughts from times to time and wants to get rid of them. Anyway, no one wants to be controlled by others’ perspectives, but CyFi is also going there to help the boy because he finds the boy pathetic and hurting. So CyFi is actually kind deep down.

u What rumor/story is starting to spread in the warehouse? How could this be the beginning of a symbol? (pg 108)

\1. The rumor is about a Akron AWOL escaping from a Juvey-cop whose tranquilizer pistol is seized and getting himself down. This big boeuf superhero may be on the way to the warehouse.

\2. This rumor or story shows the unwinds admire Connor’s courage and achievements, which would definitely be the beginning of the symbol of Roland’s hostility towards him.

u How does Risa’s opinion of Connor change after the incident with Roland in the bathroom? Is she seeing him differently than she did before? (pgs 114-116)

Risa’s opinion towards Connor actually changes. Before the incident she found Connor too impulsive to respond to Roland wisely. However, Connor controlled himself perfectly in the bathroom with no anger fled out. Although Risa is still blaming Connor for not saving him directly, but she must have found out that Connor behaved much better than she had expected. I believe Connor is more admirable to Risa from now on. Also, Risa realizes her true attitude towards Connor at that time—this boy is a hero, although she may not like him for so many troubles he has caused. Risa obviously appreciates Connor’s conscience and calmness now. Long before, it was just of being annoyed and normal cooperating.

explore unwind part 2

Part TWO:

u Why aren’t there pictures in the newspaper of the AWOLs? Is this a good or bad thing? (pages 43-44)

\1. The signal of “no pictures” shows that the cops do not have the usual intension of tracking them and then taking them to get unwound anymore. This time the cops are definitely facing a special case in which they probably want to end up the three AWOLs’ life or kill the two kidnappers, following the order of Lev’s rich family.

\2. This is definitely a bad thing because at least Risa and Connor now are facing a much scarier destiny of being killed rather than just getting unwound.

u What are Connor’s three emergency modes? Which one does he use on page 47?

\1. What is different from Connor and others is that he always has one more emergency codes compared to the common ones namely “Fight” and “Flight”, which is “Screw Up Royally”.

\2. What he uses on page 47 is the particular one: “Screw Up Royally”. In the context, it is explained as “a dangerous mental short circuit” and Connor’s previous short circuit of saving Lev is mentioned before introducing his current action towards the storked family. The author aims to clarify the same feeling of “the brain starts to fry” to show that Connor is using the same mode again.

u What happens to Lev on page 52? Why are his feelings so conflicted?

\1. Lev moves one seat forwards to put more distance between he and the two “kidnappers”. He then reaches into the backpack of a boy absorbed into music sitting next to him, pulling out a notebook and pen, writing down the “distress signal” of being kidnapped. However, the boy is furious towards Lev’s using his stationary without asking therefore ignores what Lev is suggesting and rips out the page without looking at it.

\2. lev thinks Risa and Connor still needs to follow the justice of being unwound to make themselves in different parts more meaningful. However, there is something in the darker recesses of his unconscious that is upsetting him. He don’t want them get killed and Lev knows the two “kidnappers” are not a danger to himself anymore.

u Why does Connor take the Storked baby? What does this tell us about his character?

\1. Because he has seen a storked baby twice on his own doorstep, which is a same situation compared with what is currently happening. That may reminds Connor of the guilty feeling of remaining hands-off and stimulate his empathy.

\2. This indicates that Connor is empathetic and soft deep down. He couldn’t bear the pain of watching new-borns dying and I admire his spirit of doing boldly what is righteous.

u Did Pastor Dan betray Levi? Why or why not? What situation is Lev in now? (pages 60-62)

\1. It depends.

\2. For Lev, Pastor Dan betrays him totally. In the past years, Pastor Dan acts just like his family, brainwashing Lev with the high idea of tithe. But now, just as Lev thinks, “led him to a cliff only to turn away at the last moment”, Pastor Dan forces Lev to convert his thoughts into brand new ones which is completely unknown to Lev. However, “betray” means to ignore your principles or beliefs in order to achieve sth or gain an advantage for yourself. Maybe Pastor Dan has never betrayed his faith. For Pastor Dan, everything all happens in a sudden that he may not have the chance to explain something that is hidden.

\3. Lev is now dealing with a mixed feeling composed of happy, scared and confused. He has made the mistake which diminishes Connor and Risa’s trust towards him meanwhile deciding to face the unknown self and make up for what he has done.

u Why do you think the teacher helps the Unwinds, even though she knows helping them is a federal crime? (pages 63-64)

\1. Because the teacher is experienced. She knows “how often compassion takes a back seat to expediency” and “kids only have this kind of desperation in their eyes when they’re going to be unwound. The teacher has seen so many miserable tragedies so she is quite empathetic. She once had the thought of these two “put myself and the school at risk”, but then understand what Connor is saying: “please be a human being”, because deep down the teacher approves that she ought to save living-beings from death.

u Where does the teacher take the Unwinds? What kind of place is it?

\1. She leads the unwinds to the exist however Risa and Connor are recognized and followed by cops. They then create a great mess, pretending to be clappers and in the boiling crowd the teacher points the way to antique shop on Fleming Street and someone called Sonia.

\2. The antique shop is in an older part of town and is quite old, with only one consumer and plenty of odd antiques.

u What does Sonia recommend Risa do to stay safe? How do Risa and Connor react? (page 74)

\1. Sonia recommends Risa to have Connor get her pregnant “again” to stay safe because an expectant mother will not be unwound and Risa will then be bought nine whole months.

\2. Both of them feel embarrassed and are silently against this idea. Risa is even annoyed by Sonia’s behaviors.

u What three other Unwinds do they meet? Why are they being unwound?

\1. The three other unwinds are not moving and look exhausted and wary.

\2. The smirker, Hayden comes from a ridiculously wealthy family however his parents got divorce and the brutal battle over him could not be resolved after two years. In the end his parents preferred getting Hayden unwound to allowing the other to have custody.

\3. The Asian girl with pink hair is Mai. As the fourth girl in her family, she is nothing to them compared with the new-born son.

\4. The big kid with a shark tattoo is Roland. He is being unwound because Roland beat up his stepfather for beating him mom who unexpectedly chose her husband’s side.

u Who is Roland? What tattoo does he have on his arm? What kind of person is he?

\1. Roland is a muscular guy hidden behind Sonia’s trapdoor with other two unwinds.

\2. He has a shark tattoo on his arm which looks scary.

\3. Roland is ambitious, aggressive and suspicious. He has a dream of being a military boeuf and is strong enough to fight against his stepfather. He is probably the least friendly unwind among the three for his looks at Connor with intense distrust when they first meet.

Recently I’m so surprised to find myself truly begin to enjoy the study life in BCA. Before, every day was just too tired since I’m comparing with others all the time and are commonly anxious. But now, I think I’ve relatively stopped myself from constant comparing through forcing myself to ignore others, and that effectively works. I’d like to do the comparative literacy in the future meanwhile I actively put much more concentration on my Mandarin classes. All the ancient works-Qian Zi Wen and poems from Li Bai, are so enjoyable that I feel like when myself know my true purpose exactly and truly want to achieve it, I would try my best towards it happily. First of all, we learnt how to breathe properly which can increase lung capacity and improve the property of our speaking. Mrs. Smith offered us a meditation time, and all of us began to being focus on breathing following the arranged pace. Then she plays a safe and sound music and starts telling soft and comforting words slowly. I remembered this one, which touched me a lot: it doesn’t matter if you play video games or watch movies. As long as you are happy, everything would be perfectly enough. It’s true one day we must face the cruel society with no sympathy, but BCA is always our home which eternally covers and loves us kids. And these two days we are studying the six vocal skills, namely enunciation, projection, pace, tone, emphasis, pitch. That is exactly I want and how helpful and great this course is! Sometimes it is difficult for me to pronounce clearly, and Mrs. Smith tell us helpful tricks like eliminating “g” and practicing pronunciation in front of a mirror while watching how our mouths acting. I believe that would definitely help me improve spoken English.

The happiest thing for this period is that I gradually achieve the goal which seemed impossible and hopeless before: enjoy all I’m studying.

review for unwind part 1

To end the bloody and long Second Civil War, also known as “The Heartland War”, a set of constitutional amendments known as “The Bill of Life” was passed: It satisfied both the Pro-life and the Pro-choice armies (everyone has the right to decide whether to have the children or not). The Bill of Life states that human life may not be touched from the moment of conception until a child reaches the age of thirteen. However, between the ages of thirteen and eighteen, a parent may choose to retroactively “abort “ a child…on the condition that the child’s life doesn’t “technically” end. The process by which a child is both terminated and yet kept alive is called “unwinding”.

Although many kids got tattoos, Connor Lassiter never even got one. Ariana’s comforting words let Connor feel better although he is unconfident towards surviving to 18 and the unwinding day will come within a week. They sat on the dangerous ledge of a freeway overpass, hidden behind an exit sign. For Connor, “life on edge is home.”, which I think anticipates his later destiny. He only took Ariana here and did not feel boiled about his parents anymore–even beyond boiled because there’s nothing more to fight about with his parents due to the done unwind order. Although Connor acted like a bad boy at school, but he was still afraid to do something really serious, like kicking-AWOL. He doesn’t know whether he had the nuts so clearly, for Connor himself, he is some kind of helpless to fight back. However Ariana’s promise made Connor raise courage so made up his mind. Ariana’s parents do not like him because he’s been in and out of disciplinary school and Connor still believed they would go together for hiding. Connor found the unwind order copy coincidently and hid his emotions to avenge. However he felt no better in making his parents suffer for three weeks and even starts to feel bad about that. Clearly, Connor still loved and cared about his parents even after the unwind order is signed and he just wanted their apologies and everything would even be ok then. But his parents’ coldness blew out Connor’s last hope and care towards them. He thought he still had Ariana to keep company, but he’s wrong. From the moment his parents signed those papers, he is alone, always, because some sweet dreams would not come true, and there’s no true understanding between Connor and Ariana.

For Risa and Connor, they both have something in common: a close or sweet trust, however along with shallow understanding and feeling hurts.

It is also very interesting to notice that Lev had the desire to be the center of attention. He believes that giving himself is the ultimate blessing.

initial contact with psychology (self-control)

Recently I’m studying a recording related to increase self-control.

firstly, sometimes we find it easy to deal with peers in school while the reverse is true when you at home with your family. That is because our self-control is limited. So I do not need to force myself to do everything I don’t like. In other words, “save” some self-control for vital stuff.

Researchers found out that gorillas can surprisingly control themselves better than high school elites when facing a table of snacks. And that is because people is often ahead: I should eat them now for they may be taken away a few minutes later, while the gorillas only care about presence. That reminds me to focus on current action to reduce disruption to self-control. The speaker also mentioned that put objects like phone that scatter your mind really helps.

And more importantly, he mentioned a common situation that happens on me. I always reward myself when making progress. However, ultimately I even become more inefficient. Note, what I should always focus on ought to be the goal only, rather than any progress made. Next time, think twice before we decide to reflex before the accomplish of task.

By the way, to control ourselves, we also need to enhance concentration. One of the good way to achieve this hope is to experience meditation for a few minutes everyday.

superhero (final version)

In the parallel universe, some well-known mythical civilizations really exist. There’s a kingdom full of gold called El Dorado, once upon the time a girl of which fell in love with the king of Shambhala, the mystical kingdom hidden behind the snowy peaks of Himalayas, so they gave birth to our superhero–Dorje.

Naturally, this young prince owns a super mixed power as a gift for connecting the two kingdoms, which is the ability to control gold when chanting Buddhist scriptures. That’s why El Dorado’s stolen wealth can always be recaptured. But he also has a secret power unknown to the others.

And by the way, you may think Dorje has got a weird name, but there’s something extraordinary about it indeed. In Tibetan, “Dorje” refers to diamond, which reflects his good traits like frugal, tenacious and intrepid. However, he is also pathetically distant and exclusive due to the loss of mother in his infancy.

I think “superhero”, this honorary title is well deserved for Dorje takes up the duty of eliminating greed rather than enjoying the joyous and carefree life in Shambhala when greedy rulers invaded El Dorado to make a fortune. And more importantly, he protects the two historic and precious civilizations by utilizing his hidden power eventually to conceal them from the world. So being a superhero not only requires power. For Dorje, he is responsible for both his subjects and ancient civilizations. All he has done makes him a legend.
