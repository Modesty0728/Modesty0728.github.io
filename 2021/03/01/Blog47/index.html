class discussion about unwind part 2

Firstly, I thought it is a bad thing that there are not pictures in the newspaper of the AWOLs because Risa and Connor are probably in the danger of being killed. However, based on my peers, I now realize that it can also be good since no report makes it easier for three of them to escape and hide. And the reason there are not reports can also be something outside the context which is referred from. Maybe the cops just don’t want to screw up their reputation and then cause consuming anxiety for citizens since they let three teenage unwinds escape in front of them.

And for the reason why Lev has such a mixed feeling on the bus to school is that He still wants Risa and Connor get unwound rather than being killed, which is kind of a ridiculous thought to us.

Connor’s attitude towards the storked baby is mentioned for many times, and Connor sees the irony and considers it ridiculous, so does not want it happens again. Although he is very kind to save the baby, it is still not a smart thing to do when facing the danger of getting unwound.

For Pastor Dan, we argued about whether he has betrayed or not. And eventually, the fact is, the concepts contradicting with each other in his heart is his responsibility as a Pastor and something really in his heart.

I asked about a question in class, which is the reason for Lev going away rather than going to the cops after the alarm is pulled. It is because he now does not want to get unwound anymore or he is confused and overwhelmed and just want to be alone and calm down? Mrs. Smith told us she would choose the latter.

There’s something I didn’t consider about the impact of the teacher. Mrs. Smith clarified that author’s intension is to reflect the real dystopia society: there are people just like the teacher who also think “unwind” is wrong but they cannot do anything.

The antique shop where Risa and Connor goes to is actually a reference to the underground railway in the 19th century which is used to hide black people and transport them to the North.

When Sonia suggests Risa to get pregnant again, Risa is surprised while Connor feels awkward.

The big kid, Roland, he’s anger is probably comes from this stepfather’s violence towards his mother. He is hostile, which is similar to Connor to some extent and that’s why he supposes that Connor is threat to his leadership. Shark represents “strong”, “powerful”, so Roland wants to show dominant through the tattoo (may because of his stepfather’s dominant). And the amazing experiences of the three unwinds hidden underneath Sonia’s trapdoor can show how crazy this world or dystopia is.
