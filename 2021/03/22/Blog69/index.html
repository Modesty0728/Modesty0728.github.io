Tried-and-true Love (secondary version)

Erich Fromm, a German social psychologist, argues that “love makes men overcome the sense of isolation and separateness, yet it permits to be himself, to remain his integrity.” In the dystopian novel Unwind, a lovely pair emerges— Risa and Connor. Their life traces overlap once the Unwind Orders are signed: they both go into exile. However, they seem to come from two planets more often than not— “isolation” and “separateness” are conspicuous. So, is it only their common goal of staying alive that binds them? Are Planet Connor and Planet Risa connected? Through reading *Unwind*, it is not hard to register that the affection between Risa and Connor springs up owing to both their similarities and differences.

One of Risa and Connor’s “few” similarities is the perfect interpretation towards the Admiral’s supreme rules—especially for “rise to the occasion”. The duo head for the bus stop tacitly at the instant when a police car appears, which indicates that they have been molded into coherent “fighting” forces that keep “running will attract attention” in mind, and meanwhile flexible enough to utilize surroundings as a cover— high-school-aged kids. Comparatively, the couple’s escaping plan is also coolly probative. Connor makes a split-second decision of pushing back cops, followed by rushing onto the interstate while Risa is the same except the details that her destination is a forest and there’s no one blocking her due to the car accident caused by Connor. In these cases, they are holding wild intension towards surviving, so when the chance comes in a hurry that could simultaneously wither away, they succeed to grab it. Clearly, Risa and Connor are naturals in finding out the first priority, which makes them excellent AWOLs. This facilitates the first step of love—mutual appreciation.

It is also very explicit to notice that the lovers both have grown fearless, which can be epitomized by the changing attitude towards unwinding. Risa shuddered when being order to “satisfy” budget cuts, almost involuntarily asking for other professions. Moreover, she was ashamed for current identity so hid and made it up on the transition bus. Similarly, Connor needed Ariana to light up his courage to face Unwind Order, and “would rather die than get a piece of an Unwind”. Connor and Risa were too intimidated by the prospect compared with what happens on them later: in Happy Jack Harvest Camp, Risa becomes a pianist while Connor ultimately walks into the unwinding room safe and sound. Having gone through a bunch of life and death tests, now they take it easy. As the old saying goes, “what doesn’t kill you makes you stronger.” Risa and Connor stand the test of time and a solid bond is formed between each other. Now they are ready for the love.

However, when it comes to the differences, will the emerging love shatters?

Well, they own distinct characteristics. It is like a battle between ice and fire when referring to “rational versus emotional”. Connor constantly causes trouble as getting into a bunch of fights at school, and adding a “troublesome” new-born to their exile. Conversely Risa is able to put her hatred aside while remaining observant towards Roland—even after the “Bathroom Event”. She is also sober enough to scan the danger of taking that new-born baby and then try her best to “wake” Connor up. It seems like whether their diverse emotions can be effectively controlled or not is the couple’s key difference and therefore their personality traits are just complementary. Nevertheless, later on their characteristics gradually interweave— Risa begins to care about Connor instead of keeping distances while Connor can amazingly control his fury. As they comprehending and then coming close to each other, yet still “remain integrity”, the seed of love sprouts.

Love doesn’t mean “identical”. The similarities between lovers can only get them prepared and qualified for love. Tried-and-true love emerges only if both sides build a bridge over any existing gap, since that demonstrates great efforts for love, rather than illusion of romance. Between Risa and Connor love obtained the sublimation and the eternal.
