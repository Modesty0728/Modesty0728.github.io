2021-03-07

story telling (second version)

​ My junior high classmates define me as a girl who is “boy-proof”. “I don’t want to be her desk-mate because she is so dour!” “I don’t know her…wait a minute, Uh I see… She is the one never talks.” “You know, my friend Modesty, she never talks to a boy!” 

 I’m born introverted but don’t want to embarrass others. Therefore, I feel so ashamed to them that I force myself to speak out, to take the first step, to change. But nothing changes and I define myself a waste of space. I feel as if everyone are trying their best to attack the injury that I struggle to hide…and they succeed. 

 One day, I’m talking to a psychologist casually about the “defect” and family. Simultaneously she has tears falling down, and I’m totally confused. She takes my hand, whispering: “My girl, your ‘defect’ only derives from your father’s frequent absence…that’s why you don’t know how to get along with boys.” At that moment, I’m relieved, but then heartbroken. So, I haven’t done anything wrong but only my plush toys know how many hours I’ve been crying. I really want to hug my earlier self and tell her “It’s okay.” But I won’t be inclined to self-pity anymore, because what doesn’t kill me makes me stronger. No one can define you. No one can decide who you are. So be yourself rather than being humble towards worthless people, whose “malice” is only ashes for a strong girl.
