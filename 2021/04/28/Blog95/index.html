Fairy Godmother (script analysis unit)

  1. Who am I?

I am a supporting character in Disney’s story Cinderella, the physical embodiment of hope. I am Cinderella’s godmother. I always appear suddenly in the thin air with twinkling lights (sparkly confetti), wearing a gown with two wings shaking behind her back and a wand (can be summoned from nothingness) at the end of which is grafted a shiny tin foil star. I’m so warm-hearted that I could not bear any unfair. So, I offer poor Cinderella a little bit of help…make her princess dream come true: a dress and a tiara. In fairy tales, I’m “a fairy with magical powers who acts as a mentor or parent to someone, in the role that an actual godparent was expected to play in many societies.” I stand for children’s fantasies about magic, and “whenever events are right for a fairy tale to recur, I steps in to make sure that the tale in question runs its course with as few fatalities as possible.” I wouldn’t appear to Ella if she loses her faith. Actually, I did not appear until Cinderella was at her weakest point. So, I won’t offer my help casually. I would only appear if Ella keeps a kind heart, which is strong enough to summon me, and just need a little bit of magic to push her to what she deserves. I like magic, Cinderella’s happiness, and books. What I dislike are hopelessness, unhappiness, and losing my wand! And I’m always in an active status. My wand holds incredible power, such as metamorphosis and the ability to warp reality among others. However, its magic is not exclusive to me as mortals can also use it (can be either good or evil however). However, despite my sweetness I do not sympathize with those who are unforgiving towards others. I also have a connection with my wand (they call it “telepathy”).

  1. Where am I?

According to Disney film version, although I would suddenly appear in the thin air, I’m always watching Cinderella secretly, caring about her and her original family (Ella’s parents both are gone now). But when Ella summons me, I would come to her wherever she is.

  1. What surrounds me?

Twinkling lights (sparkly confetti) is always surrounding me when I show up out of thin air. Besides, Cinderella is with me, together with two white horses, a driver, a footman, and a carriage which are produced by me.

  1. What time is it?

In the evening. After Cinderella’s abusers left. Before the ball gets started.

  1. What are my circumstances?

I’m equipped with a magical wand. I was appointed be to Cinderella’s fairy godmother. I am summoned by Cinderella since she hasn’t lost her faith even when she is at her weakest point.

  1. What are my relationships?

I’m Cinderella’s godmother. As a fairy godmother, I am also children’s beacon of hope.

  1. What do I want?

I want Cinderella to go to the ball. I want Cinderella happy. I want Cinderella to always keep her faith.

  1. What is my obstacle?

My precious wand can also be used by bad people. When magic is used as dark force, I would probably lose my job!! And the whole world would get messed up. So, my wand is my pressure, but also my obstacle as well. What’s more, I only have the magic to show up out of the air and summon my wand without using my pressure. Besides this obstacle, I also “tend to be comically absent-minded, continuously loses sight of the obvious, and has a habit of both losing her wand and forgetting her magic words “Bibbidi-Bobbidi-Boo“.

Cinderella’s abusers are also big obstacles. They oppress my dear girl and nearly ruin my contribution and Ella’s sweet future in the last minute.

  1. What do I do to get what I want?

To get Ella happier, I show up in time to offer her nice dress. Being aware of the stepmother’s selfishness, I was overt to make a pair of magical glass slippers as a back-up plan…and that is eventually proved to be profound and significant.
