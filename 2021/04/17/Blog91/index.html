final monologue analysis (stoker)

  1. Who am I?

I am Evelyn, a housewife with perfect posture. I’m distant from my daughter India and my husband Richard. I’m quite jealous about the bond between Richard and India as they go hunting together all day and what I only can do is to get their preys stored. I’m alcoholic. Actually, drinking helps me escape from life and the jealousy.

  1. Where am I?

Always at my big house, with Stokers.

  1. What surrounds me?

A luxury house. Loads of servants. My daughter India is always with me although keeping distance with me. My husband leaves me but his brother Charlie comes who lets me feel the sense of romance once again.

  1. What time is it?

I don’t know…

  1. What are my circumstances?

I lose my husband on my daughter India’s 18th birthday. It was a great shock… however, I am an unstable woman, and the sudden presence of my husband Richard’s brother—Charlie is much to my delight! Charlie recalls my memory of young Richard. Gradually, Charlie and me grow intimate as India refuses to get rid of melancholy and relax. Actually, my daughter is so distant…You know, it is just because I’m so lonely deep down and find it hard to get close to Stokers. I admit that I am just vulnerable like other woman, eager to get my husband’s company and love. On a beautiful evening, India’s great aunt Gwendolyn makes a visit to our house which is very much to my dismay. She suggests me to pay attention to Charlie and once again speaks of my dead husband Richard. I was flattered by the emerging romance so ignored what she was saying and was sensitive and a little bit guilty when my husband was mentioned… But all of that does not influence my more and more intensified feeling for Charlie at all. So, can you ever imagine how astonished it is to witness my own daughter grows closer to Charlie and eventually intimate? My heart is full of hatred. I burnt out everything from my husband and the preys hunted by my daughter, and then coldly expresses the desire to watch India suffer before confronting Charlie. I have already “realized” what happened to India’s big aunt and Mrs, McGarrick. I only ask for Charlie to get my daughter go. But once again, I’m totally intrigued by Charlie’s charm and he tries to seduce me with his belt suddenly! He calls India to come here, and my dear daughter is with a rifle. I thought she would kill me…but Charlie is shot.

  1. What are my relationships?

India is the daughter if Richard and me although both of them seem distant from me. The appearance of Richard’s brother Charlie makes me satisfied since for the first time India can never steal love from me anymore. Later, I do find Charlie suspicious, but his great charm still steals away my ration.

  1. What do I want?

I always want to be cared and loved by the man. I want to get closer to my husband and daughter. I want to get back to the time that Richard is still intimate with me. Later on, after discovering the relationship between Charlie and India, I want India, my own daughter, to suffer. In the end, I want Charlie to stay away from India.

  1. What is my obstacle?

My husband leaves me so there’s no chance to rebuild our relationship again. My daughter is absolutely a “Stoker”. The stokers enjoy the satisfaction of killing, which makes it impossible for me to get closer to them. And for my wish to let India suffer…I do hate her for what she has done…what I still love her. India is my hope. She is the reason why I had a child. I want to utilize her to improve my relationship with my husband. And what kind of person Charlie really is rejects my hope towards love.

  1. What do I do to get what I want?

In order to get closer to my husband, I had a child. And to be loved and accompanied by a man, I build up a close relationship to Charlie. In the end, I try to warn Charlie to get away from my daughter by talking and warning.
