Revolution of the Ocean (final version)

Darwin started studying artificial selection as early as the nineteenth century, feeding and experimenting on pigeons which were selected according to individual traits (Hooper, 2017). Obviously, human beings have been exploring this field for centuries and their curiosity continues. After adapting to the environment, normally the advantageous living beings would be chosen to provide people with a more satisfying life. The genetically modified organisms, also known as GMOs, are one of the profound products or examples of artificial selection of which “genetic materials are artificially manipulated” according to the definition of GMO. In other words, GMOs are created “against nature”. Our research focuses on the first consuming-allowed bioengineered animal ever—GM salmon (also known as AquAdvantage salmon) created by AquaBounty Technologies (Nordrum, 2020).

This biotechnology enlarges salmon’s size (comparatively a doubled size in a certain growing period), and the time they take to grow to market size is halved compared with conventional Atlantic salmon which are definitely slow growers with a 32 to 36 months’ life circle approximately (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.).

So far, only a few countries have been using this method to improve salmon’s quality, e.g. US, Canada, and Argentina. The first transgenic salmon came down in pike in 1989, Canada, which could grow all year around rather than just in spring and summer (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.). On November 19th, 2015, with food and environmental safety both ensured, AquaBounty’s genetically modified salmon came into season with the approval of the US Food and Drug Administration (FDA). People are gradually accepting genetically modified salmon.

So, what are the details about this “delicious” technology? AquAdvantage salmon are originally Atlantic salmon inserted another two special genes from the chinook salmon (Oncorhynchus tshawytscha) and Ocean Pout. Generally, the scientists transfer the growth hormone (or growth promoting gene) into fertilized Atlantic salmon eggs. Therefore, the original (Atlantic) ones manage to grow faster in warmer seawater. All ultimate products are sterile females (The Fish Site, 2010), which directly diminishes the concern of them breeding with wild salmon. Wenonah Hauter, the chairman of Food and Water Watch, also came up with disapprovals based on salmon’s wild competition ability. Once again, it has been backfired by Dr. Eric Hallerman who claims that GM salmon are domesticated (The Fish Site, 2010). Current results also indicate that AquAdvantage salmon are less competitive than their “ancestors” because of the low quantity requirement of food resources. However, although it finally became true that FDA approved the GM salmon, about 60 retailers (combined with 4,660 stores) refuse to have GM salmon sold in their stores (Shreeves, 2019) as GMO labels are never required on AquaBounty Salmon—consumers could be unaware of the particularity of what they have just eaten. It is also being said that AquAdvantage salmon contain less nutrient—“producing less of the beneficial omega—3 fatty acids than non-GMO salmon” (Say NO to GMO Salmon + Full-Length Feature Online: Salmon Confidential, 2013). There are worries based on the survival of wild animals and human health indeed, but undeniably AquAdvantage salmon benefit the environment. For instance, they actually improve the conservation of wild fish populations. As aforementioned, the demand for food decreases. That sustains the aquaculture effectively. Moreover, where GM salmon are bred is very close to the grocery store, which lowers carbon emissions during transportation. AquaBounty is also perfectly capable of recycling waste water and resources (Nine Things You Need To Know About GMO Salmon, n.d.).

“GMOs” has always been a controversial topic. In science class, we talked about its pros and cons. One of the biggest concerns is about GMO’s uncertainty in the future. Through this meaningful research, we see how amazingly a new genetic product came out, and how scientists argue, raising up questions, analyzing, researching…We can only say that up to now the AquAdvantage salmon are relatively friendly to the earth. No matter what would happen on this brand-new species, we should always appreciate and continue the great efforts put in this field which promote the development of genetics.
