Nuclear Power Plants on First Nation Conclusion

\1. Main clash

l Safety issue

Nuclear actually indicates the least security risks.

You would say that we cannot ignore a single possibility, since 1% possibility can cause irreversible health damage to living beings.

->Rebuttal:

the possibility of being involved in a car accident is much higher than nuclear leak.

Question: So, do you want to change a topic? What about this one: would FP risk their lives to take a bus?

Only if there is 1% possibility to be risky in safety industry, the “1% possibility” is not concerning.

l Culture issue

When we decide to build nuclear power plants in FP nations, we would worry about their culture heritage spontaneously. It is a concerning issue indeed. It is something we need to think and worry about. We need to build the sense to protect their culture and then make our determination visible. How? Through government legislation. But that’s not what we can worry about. Now the environmental department needs to get in charge and carry one our resolution.

->Rebuttal:

If you are so active caring about the loss of native culture, ok your battle target is the environmental department of the government, not us. When we come to the disappear of Malaysia Airlines Flight 370, which one is to blame: the pilot, or the Wright brothers? Similarly, are we the ones to blame?

Nuclear power plants are not responsible for protecting native culture.

l Economy issue

Nuclear plants require lower cost.

->In this way, benefit national economy.

->better protection

if\ you question whether the money would be used on protection project, (I can tell you that whether or not to do so is also related to legislation rather than the construction of plants. if you wanna get into the topic of legislation, infinite questions would follow, and I bet you can answer none of them) Why don’t you ask more questions like “whether the legislators are holding justice?”, or “will the legislator use the money to buy thousands of hamburgers if they are starving?”

l Environment issue

Without nuclear energy, FP do continuous logging. And that is so beneficial to the environment! FP should be thankful for being offered a convenient chance of using nuclear energy

\2. Location necessary

è Near sea

!! We need tons of cold water to cool down the fuel rods inside the nuclear reactors

*世界超半数所有核反应堆都在海边，不在海边的周围也有大量水源。

*日本核泄漏后导致爆炸的主要原因就是未及时注入冷水，可见cool water的重要性！！

！！wind energy: toxic gases dissipate in the atmosphere (苏联切尔诺贝利事件有毒粉尘飘到了瑞典)

è Remote

Everyone is equal indeed. So, which one means less damage: kill one person or kill a country of person?

->I know you would say, “what happened in Japan indicates that the damage is going to people around the world, not just the people living near plants!”

->ok, so you are even confused about the basic facts: Japanese could totally choose to bury those waste water deep down into ground, which is one of the two ways of dealing with nuclear leak (another one is discharging it into ocean) Japan chose the former due to the latter’s huge opportunity cost.
