Dulce et Decorum Est

Bent double, like old beggars under sacks,

Knock-kneed, coughing like hags, we cursed through sludges,

Till on the haunting flares we turned our backs,

And towards our distant rest began to trudge.

Men marched asleep. Many had lost their boots,

But limped on, blood-shod. All went lame; all blind;

Drunk with fatigue; deaf even to the hoots

Of gas-shells dropping softly behind.

Frozen air exploded—the broken world in torpedo sound![M1]

Organs fled[M2] faster than time.[M3]

A man defend’d a ring b’fore being ground[M4] [M5]

What a romantic mime.

Scarlet killed[M6] [1]indigo:[M7]

I saw the fractured soul—float for a mile.[M8] [M9]

In all my dreams, they do not let me go—[M10] [M11]

Hound[M12] me—[2]unrecognizable friends, who’s guile?[M13] [M14]

A butcher once, now huband—slaughtered[M15] , end.[M16]

Sky bleeds[M17] , corrupt bones dance[M18] —call for the dead.[M19] [M20]

[3]If time prefers, resurgence, Jack and Rose[M21] [M22] [M23]

Enjoy again, by skull of wraith[M24] sable cods snigger[M25] .[M26]

Would Jack rots in [4]the sea of bloody rose? [M27] [M28]

[5]Fiery dowry: torn ring stenches on mutilated finger[M29]

[6]Would love fail Jack—abused to cover crime?[M30] [M31] [M32]

Bone-pierced[M33] billow freezes boiling[M34] blood, and fire of zest[M35] for the war.[M36]

With sordid [7]specimen brow of quick lime[M37] —[M38] [M39]

[8]sludges smother fuzzy flesh wreathing[M40] itself around iron bar,­­­­­­—[M41]

My friends, you would not tell with such high zest

To children ardent for some desperate glory,

The old Lie: Dulce et decorum est

Pro patria mori
